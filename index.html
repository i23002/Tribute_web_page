Who is David J Malan?

Professor Malan is an American computer scientist and is a “Gorden Mckay Professor” of Computer Science at Harvard University. He is best known for teaching the course CS50, which is the largest open-learning course at Harvard University and Yale University. CS50 is the largest “Massive OpenLearning Course”. According to Wikipedia lectures viewed by over a million people on the edxplatform up to 2017.

What is CS50?

Computer Science 50 also known as CS50 is an on campus as well as online introductory computerscience course at Harvard and Yale University. According to Wikipedia “In the year 2016, CS50 became available to high school students as an 'Advanced Placement Computer Science' course. “ The on campus version is Harvard’s largest class with over 500+ students , 100+ stuffs , and up to 2,200 participants in their hackathons. As of 2022 the online version widely known as CS50x teaches the language C , Python , SQL, HTML, CSS and JavaScript. It also teaches some of fundamental concepts of computer science like ARRAY’S and DATA STRUCTURES as well as one of the latest frameworks Flask.

Malan and CS50

In the year 1996, when malan enrolled at Harvard , initially studying government took CS50 in the fall of 1996. At that time CS50 was taught by “Brian Kernighan”. Inspired by him malan began his education in computer science and graduated in the year 1999. After working on different fields malan began teaching CS50 and with the help of latest technologies and everyone’s initiative the online version of CS50 quickly stole the spotlight. Now in 2022 CS50 is a world famous introductory computer science course. With an amazing team of CS50 and a brilliant teacher like David.J.Malan, students who were not able to get into Harvard or those who had feared to to study CS are now able to write not Just one single line of “Hello World” but also able to create their own single Web applications, Projects, and many more.
